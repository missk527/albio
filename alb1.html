ALB1. Biological Molecules

Key Notes (Synced with Video)

0:00 - 0:24 | Introduction to Biological Molecules
  • This video introduces biological molecules and why they are important in living organisms.
  • It covers the four main types of biological molecules.
  • It explains monomers, polymers, condensation reactions, and hydrolysis reactions.
  • These ideas help explain how large, complex molecules are built and broken down in cells.
0:25 - 1:11 | Four Main Types and Carbon
  • The four main types of biological molecules are carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, and nucleic acids.
  • They are called organic molecules because they contain carbon.
  • Carbon can form four covalent bonds with other carbon atoms or different elements.
  • Carbon bonds can be single or double bonds, allowing many complex structures to form.
  • Covalent bonds are strong because electrons are shared between atoms.
1:12 - 1:50 | Elements in Biological Molecules
  • All four main biological molecules contain carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen.
  • Carbohydrates contain only carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen.
  • Lipids, proteins, and nucleic acids can contain extra elements.
  • Proteins always contain nitrogen.
  • Nucleic acids always contain nitrogen and phosphorus.
1:51 - 3:31 | Monomers, Polymers, and Polymerisation
  • Monomers are small basic units that join together to form larger molecules.
  • In carbohydrates, the monomers are monosaccharides, such as glucose.
  • In proteins, the monomers are amino acids.
  • In nucleic acids, the monomers are nucleotides.
  • Polymers are large molecules made from many repeating monomer subunits.
  • Carbohydrate polymers are polysaccharides, such as glycogen.
  • Protein polymers are polypeptides, made from many amino acids.
  • Nucleic acid polymers are polynucleotides, such as DNA strands.
  • Polymerisation is the process where monomers join to form polymers.
  • Lipids are made from subunits like glycerol and fatty acids, but they are not true polymers because they are not made of repeating units.
3:32 - 4:26 | Condensation Reactions
  • Condensation reactions are used to synthesise, or make, many polymers.
  • In a condensation reaction, water is removed to form a chemical bond between molecules.
  • A hydroxyl group from one molecule and a hydrogen atom from another molecule are removed.
  • The removed parts form a molecule of water.
  • Energy is required for condensation reactions, and this energy is supplied by ATP.
  • When amino acids join to form a polypeptide, one water molecule is released for each additional amino acid added.
4:27 - 5:02 | Hydrolysis Reactions
  • Hydrolysis reactions break polymers or large molecules, such as lipids, into their subunits.
  • Water is used to break a chemical bond between molecules.
  • Splitting water provides the H and OH groups needed to separate the subunits.
  • Hydrolysis reactions release energy.
  • A polypeptide can be broken down into amino acids by hydrolysis.
